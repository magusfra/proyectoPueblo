Church of Santa Eugenia

It is the coffin of Fray Domingo de Santa María. Born in the middle of the 16th century. Dominican. He took the habit in the convent of San Estaban de Salamanca and was master of novices. Delicate in health, he was sent to Astudillo to see if he would improve with the change of scenery, but he died (1598). When his sister died ten years later, when they buried her, they realized that Fray Domingo's body was incorrupt. Asking for a license from the ecclesiastical authorities, they took him out


Church of San Pedro

The church of San Pedro is a Catholic temple in the town of Astudillo. Built between the 15th and 16th centuries, its architecture is Gothic-Renaissance in style, although it retains some earlier elements, from the 13th century.


Arch of San Martin

The Arco de San Martín is located in the center of the Palencia municipality of Astudillo, and specifically in the square of the same name, that is to say, in the Plaza de San Martín and it is one of the oldest monuments that preserved in said municipality, after the creation of the Castillo de la Mota the medieval wall was built dating from the 13th to the 14th century.


Castle of La Mota

The ruins of the Astudillo castle rise up on an outstanding speck broken off from the San Martín páramo. Although its origin could date back to the 10th century, there is no documentary evidence of its existence until the 11th. Possibly it was erected to reinforce the important fortress of Castrojeriz and would play an important role in the confrontations between Fernando I and Vermudo III for control of these lands near the Pisuerga river, whose course separated the kingdoms of Castilla y León. The castle of La Mota will also suffer the turbulent years of the reign of María de Molina (lady of Astudillo) and Juan II (who will spend long periods of time in this town).
